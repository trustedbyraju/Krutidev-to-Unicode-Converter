Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Krutidev to Unicode conversion.

Krutidev (also known as Kruti Dev) is a popular ANSI-based Hindi font widely used in India since the 1990s, especially in government offices, newspapers, and courts. It works by mapping Devanagari characters to ASCII/ANSI byte values — meaning the actual data stored is English characters, not Hindi. This makes Krutidev documents incompatible with modern systems, unsearchable by Google, and unreadable without the Krutidev font installed. Converting to Unicode solves all these problems permanently.
Our converter uses a meticulously crafted character mapping table that covers all Krutidev characters including vowels, consonants, matras (vowel signs), half-characters, nukta variants, punctuation, and special symbols. Accuracy for standard Krutidev 010 text is 99%+. Edge cases involving rare conjuncts or mixed fonts may require manual review.
